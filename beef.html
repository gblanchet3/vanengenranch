Beef with a backstory — from one specific animal, on one specific ranch, to your kitchen.

How they're raised

Grass, sun, and time.

Our cattle live the way cattle were meant to live — on open pasture, eating grass that grew where they're standing.

No feedlot finish. No grain-heavy push to market weight. We let the animals grow at the pace the land sets, which is slower than the commodity model and produces beef that tastes like the place it came from.

Because we're veterinarians, we know every animal — its history, its temperament, its health. That's not a marketing line; it's just how a small herd works when the people running it grew up reading bloodwork.

"From pasture, not a feedlot. From us, not a warehouse."

From ranch to your freezer

Three steps. That's it.

Reserve a share

You commit to a whole, half, or quarter share before harvest. We pair you to a specific animal.

Cut to your kitchen

We work with you on the cut sheet — steaks, roasts, ground, however you cook. Our butcher handles processing.

Pick up, fill the freezer

You pick up directly at the butcher. Minimal freezer time on our end. Maximum freshness on yours.

Share options

Pick your share.

Each share is part of one animal raised on our pasture. Pricing is finalized closer to harvest — these are the formats.

Quarter share

The Sampler

~85–110 lbs of beef

Steaks, roasts, ground, and the rest — proportional to a quarter animal. A solid year of beef for one or two people.

Half share

The Family

~170–220 lbs of beef

The most popular size. A full mix of cuts, enough to feed a household for a year and share a few packages with friends.

Whole share

The Commitment

~340–440 lbs of beef

The whole animal, your cut sheet entirely. Best for large families, multiple households splitting, or restaurants that want one source.
