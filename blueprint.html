Before we build

Six questions
that change everything.

The strategy above is solid. But the details below determine the entire product, pricing, logistics, and fulfillment model. These are the answers we need in our first real working session.

01
Inventory & Timing
When do animals go to slaughter — and roughly how many this year?
This sets the entire pre-order timeline and tells us how aggressive the marketing push needs to be. Fall? Spring? One batch or multiple?
02
Product Format
Are we selling whole / half / quarter animals, individual cuts, or all of the above?
This is the single biggest decision. It determines pricing, the storefront, fulfillment complexity, and how we talk to customers. Whole animals are simpler. Cuts are more accessible.
03
Pricing
What's the target price per pound or per animal for DTC?
We need to know the margin we're building toward before we set up a single order form or write a single price on the site.
04
Processing
Who is your butcher — and how does that relationship currently work?
Nick's instinct to avoid a big freezer operation is smart. A pre-order model solves it — but only if we know the processing lead times and who controls the cut sheet.
05
Content & Brand
Nick: how comfortable are you being the face of this on camera?
The Instagram content is already authentic and real. If Nick leans in, that's a massive asset. If not, we build the brand around the land and the product. Both work — but differently.
06
First Customers
Can we activate the friend network as founding customers — right now?
Gabe's network bought before. If a handful commit to a pre-order in the next two weeks, we have proof of concept and a list to build from. Who's the first call?

Partnership options

Two ways to
do this together.

Gabe handles the commercial and marketing side — brand, site, CRM, pre-orders, social strategy. Here's how the partnership can be structured.

Clean & Simple
Flat Retainer
  • 3-day setup sprint — one-time fee (TBD)
  • Monthly retainer to keep eyes on the operation
  • Predictable cost, no complexity
  • Gabe owns the commercial buildout start to finish
  • Retainer drops as Nick/Kristen take over operations
Best fit if you want a clean vendor relationship with no strings attached. Simple, professional, done.
Skin in the Game
Setup + Profit Share
  • Reduced setup fee — Gabe shares the early risk
  • Small % of incremental margin over commodity baseline
  • Gabe's incentive is perfectly aligned with yours
  • Light monthly oversight — Gabe wants this to work
  • Measured against a clear baseline (current $/head)
Best fit if you want a true partner, not a vendor. The better this does, the better we all do.
