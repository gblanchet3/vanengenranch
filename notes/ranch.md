## How the cattle are raised

This section is the substance of the brand story. Use directly for site copy.

### Pasture-raised, all the way through
Life starts on pasture. Develops on pasture. Calves stay on mother through ~September, then transition to grass on their own.

### Grass-started, grain-finished
We start on grass and finish on grain. We say it plainly because it's true and because the eating experience is better:

- **Fat profile** is richer and more consistent.
- **Flavor** finishes well — grass-only finish doesn't develop the same way.
- **Efficiency** matters too — finishing on grain gets the animal to the right weight without dragging out the timeline.

This is a deliberate choice, not a compromise. Some buyers will look for "100% grass-fed" — we're not that, and we're confident in why.

### Veterinary care
Both Nick and Kristen are practicing vets. Antibiotics and pharmaceutical interventions are minimal and used only when an animal needs them. Full mineral and vitamin supplementation. Healthy herd, honest food.

### Not certified organic
Organic certification is a different set of regulatory hoops we've chosen not to jump through. It doesn't change how the animals are raised — but we don't claim what we haven't certified.

### Rotational grazing
Stocked at ~1.2 cows/acre. We move the herd to follow the grass. Manure goes back into the soil, carbon stays where it belongs. Winter foraging on residue feed.

### Breed
**Angus-derived beef program.** We started with Wagyu-influenced genetics and have shifted toward Angus because they finish more efficiently. Future plans (2028+) include reintroducing Wagyu F1 from our breeding program — but for the foreseeable future, all Van Engen beef is Angus.

We're not offering Wagyu via partner ranches in v1. When Wagyu comes to our beef program, it'll be ours.

## Product format (v1)

**Quarter / Half / Whole shares only.** No individual cuts. Volume is too small (10–50 head/year for the foreseeable future) to justify the operational complexity of individual-cut DTC.

## Pre-order model

Pre-orders with deposits up front:

- Tells us how many to harvest before any animal goes to processing
- Solves the cash flow problem (cash in hand before we incur the $1,700/head processing cost)
- Solves the freezer-storage problem (no inventory sitting around)
- Lets us decide late whether to pull from partner ranches for fall '26 to fulfill demand
